Achievable security toolkit!

The Electronic Frontier Foundation has a program called Surveillance Self-Defence, which helps you understand and avoid surveillance online.

If you only do one thing, use 2FA

Use two factor authentication (also called multi-factor authentication, or 2FA) where you can, but especially on important services like your email account, your bank, and places where you store the things you care most about (your home address, your photos, etc).

There's a great run-down on multi-factor authentication here.

Passwords and password managers

Getting a password manager will help you set more secure passwords (long ones!), and means you don't have to remember them all. It's explained well here.

Have you been hacked?

You can check your email to see if any of your logins have been stolen or compromised, by going to haveibeenpwned.com. There are instructions on what to do if you're email comes up on a hacked list.

If you know you have had photos stolen, you should check out BaddAssArmy.org.
