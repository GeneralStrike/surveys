Your privacy

To meet the aim of these surveys, I need you to be as honest as you can. To make that happen, the survey is as anonymous as I can make it and I've explained a few things below, so you can feel comfortable.

What information do I collect?

I collect the informaton you give me in the survey itself (your answers), and a little bit of logging information, such as the time you completed it and your IP address (the number your internet connection uses to identify itself on the internet).

I'm not collecting your name, email, other contact details. The IP address is not linked to the survey you submit, and isn't in the survey data.

What will I do with it?

The survey answers you give will be used to find trends, and will be presented to the public.

I'll also make the data available to researchers, but only after I check through to make sure there are no personal identfiers or hints in the data.

Will I be able to identify you?

Mostly no, but there's something you should know about the internet!

Because the server collects your IP address and the time you open the survey, and the survey itself collects the time you submit, I would be able to see which IP address goes with which survey, if there's only one person submitting at a time.

Your IP address could then tell me where you were when you submitted if I decided to look, and if I had access to the sort of information owned by governments, internet service providers and Google (I don't though!). This is true of pretty much every website you ever visit.

But Keira, that sounds concerning!

It does, doesn't it? You can protect yourself when you are browsing by using TOR, or a VPN.

The purpose of the logs is to make sure the site is working properly, and to give me a warning if someone is trying to attack it. I promise to delete the logs (where the IP address is stored) as soon as the survey open period is open, and never use them to identify you.

What rights do you have over your survey after you submit?

None.

This is because once you submit, I have no way to know for sure that you actually sumitted a survey, or which one was yours. That means I can't amend it for you if you want to change an answer, and can't delete your answers for you if you want me to.

For that reason, I ask that you are careful not to include your name, contact details, or any other identifying info. If I see such information while I'm processing the data I'll do my best to remove it in any case.

Cookies

This site doesn't put tracking cookies on your device.

I'm not using any analytics tools, tracking tools, ads, etc. There is no authentication (you do't log in), so I'm not using any cookies associated with that either.

The surveys are built in Django, and use a CSRF token, which is not used for tracking.
